The "dynamo-electric machine" employed self-powering electromagnetic field coils rather than permanent magnets to create the stator field.

The use of electromagnets rather than permanent magnets greatly increased the power output of a dynamo and enabled high power generation for the first time.

Large power generation dynamos are now rarely seen due to the now nearly universal use of alternating current for power distribution.

A coil of wire rotating in a magnetic field produces a current which changes direction with each 180Â° rotation, an alternating current (AC).

Through a series of discoveries, the dynamo was succeeded by many later inventions, especially the AC alternator, which was capable of generating alternating current.
